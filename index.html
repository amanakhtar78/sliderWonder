AD 600

Chichén Itzá

Chichén Itzá is a pre-Columbian archaeological site located in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico. It was a major focal point in the Northern Maya Lowlands from the Late Classic (c. AD 600–900) through the Terminal Classic (c. AD 800–900) and into the early portion of the Postclassic period (c. AD 900–1200).

AD 1931

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It was completed in 1931 and stands 30 meters tall, excluding its 8-meter pedestal. The statue is located at the peak of the 700-meter Corcovado mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park, overlooking the city of Rio de Janeiro.

AD 80

Colosseum

The Colosseum, originally known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient amphitheater located in the center of Rome, Italy. Built of travertine limestone, tuff (volcanic rock), and brick-faced concrete, it is the largest amphitheater ever built and is considered one of the greatest works of Roman architecture and engineering.

2560 BC

Giza Pyramids

The Giza Pyramids, located on the Giza Plateau near Cairo, Egypt, are among the most famous and recognizable structures in the world. Built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom of Egypt, they were constructed as tombs for the Pharaohs Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure. The Great Pyramid of Giza, built by Khufu, is the largest of the three.

700 BC

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, generally built along an east-to-west line across the historical northern borders of China to protect the Chinese states and empires against the raids and invasions of the various nomadic groups of the Eurasian Steppe.

AD 1450

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, renowned for its well-preserved architecture and panoramic views. It was built in the 15th century AD and later abandoned, likely due to the Spanish Conquest. Machu Picchu was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 and is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

312 BC

Petra

Petra is an ancient city located in Jordan that was the capital of the Nabatean Kingdom. It is famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Petra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and has been described as one of the most precious cultural properties of man's cultural heritage.

AD 1643

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture, a style that combines elements from Persian, Ottoman, Indian, and Islamic architectural styles.
